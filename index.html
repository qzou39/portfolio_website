About Me

I am a senior at Georgia Institute of Technology. I double-majored in biomedical engineering and psychology. I am very much certain that BME is what I want to pursue. It probably has something to do with the fact that my whole family are engineers. The first real toy they bought me was a fruit-power battery experiment kit for kids, which came with some tiny light bulbs and a lab manual for experiments. I wasn’t satisfied with merely seeing those color changes of bioelectric light bulbs - I wanted to know the biochemical and physiological functions, anatomical and histological structures, epidemiology, and pharmacology, and that started my interest in biomedical science.

Psychology, on the other hand, has made me think deeper about what is reliable and what isn’t. My reflection on reliability has made me appreciate engineering, for its precision and pragmaticism. My experience in psychology taught me that I have made the right choice in pursuing biomedical engineering.
